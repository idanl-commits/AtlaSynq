FAQ

Common questions

Everything you need to know about governed AI agents for enterprise.

What is AtlaSynq?

AtlaSynq is an enterprise platform that deploys governed AI agents for every business department — Finance, Sales, Engineering, HR, Marketing, and Legal. Each agent is purpose-built for its team's context, integrated with your existing tools, and enforces role-based access control (RBAC) by default. Unlike generic AI chatbots, AtlaSynq agents know your data scope, your policies, and your integrations.

How does AtlaSynq enforce governance on AI agents?

AtlaSynq applies RBAC policies at the agent layer. Admins define what data each role can access, which integrations are permitted, and what actions agents may take. These policies are enforced before every response — so a Finance agent can never access Engineering's codebase, and a junior analyst can't query restricted board data. Every action is logged for audit.

Which AI models does AtlaSynq support?

AtlaSynq supports GPT-4o (OpenAI), Claude 3.5 (Anthropic), Gemini 1.5 Pro (Google), Llama 3 (Meta), GitHub Copilot, and Perplexity. You can assign a different model per department, per role, or even per query type. Governance policies apply uniformly regardless of which model is selected.

What is the typical ROI of deploying AtlaSynq?

Customers typically recover 30% of manual information-gathering hours within the first quarter. For a 50-person knowledge-work team spending 5 hours/week on research at an $80k average salary, that's approximately 3,900 hours and $150,000 saved annually. Payback periods typically range from 1–3 months. Use our ROI calculator to model your specific numbers.

Which integrations does AtlaSynq support?

AtlaSynq connects to Salesforce, HubSpot, Jira, GitHub, Slack, QuickBooks, Notion, Google Drive, Stripe, Gong, LinkedIn, Zendesk, Greenhouse ATS, NetSuite, Microsoft Teams and more — all via OAuth and secure API. Integration discovery is automatic: when you select a department role, AtlaSynq detects which tools your team uses and proposes a connection plan.

Is AtlaSynq secure and compliant for enterprise use?

Yes. AtlaSynq is SOC 2 Type II compliant with end-to-end encryption in transit and at rest. We support SSO/SAML, have a full audit log for every agent action, and never train on your company data. Data residency options (US, EU) are available on enterprise plans. Our security documentation is available on request.
